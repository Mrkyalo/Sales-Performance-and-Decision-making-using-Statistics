# Sales-Performance-and-Decision-making-using-Statistics
## Project overview
This project analyzes sales data across different store types,regions and marketing campaigns.
The dataset spans multiple months and includes units sold,revenue and campaign activity.
The goal of this project is to provide data driven business recommendations.

## Statistical summary

- **Standard Deviation:** =4,280 
- **Range:** =26,886 (large spread between min and max)

- **Kurtosis:** 0.61

## Key Insights
1. **Channel Performance**
   - Online stores generate higher revenue, especially in Nairobi and Central regions.
   - Physical stores provide more consistent baseline sales.

2. **Regional Trends**
   - Nairobi and Central regions show strong performance.
   - Rift Valley and Coast regions are more volatile.

3. **Marketing Campaigns**
   - Campaign days correlate with higher sales.

4. **Seasonality**
   - Peaks occur mid-month and end-of-month, likely tied to salary cycles.
   - Stronger demand observed in May, June, and December.

5. **Outliers**
   - Several zero-sales days suggest stockouts, closures, or system issues

#  Recommendations
- **Targeted Campaigns:** Focus marketing in Rift Valley and Coast to stabilize performance.  
- **Inventory Management:** Investigate zero-sales days and align stock with peak demand cycles.  
- **Channel Strategy:** Leverage online channels in Nairobi and Central; boost physical store promotions.  
- **Personalization:** Tailor promotions by region and store type. (outliers)
- **Monitoring:** Track ROI of campaigns and maximise on what is working
